## LinkedIn

### #1 Format: PDF Carousel (Document Post)

- **Engagement:** 6.60% average ER — #1 format on the platform (Dataslayer, April 2026)
- **Why it works:** Every swipe signals dwell time → algorithmic re-serves. Carousels earn dramatically higher save rates than video or text — users treat them as reference content, extending half-life from hours to weeks.
- **Tone shift:** Conversational tone is overtaking thought-leadership grandstanding. "Posts that work are not 'thought leadership,' they're conversations. The best-performing posts are written like DMs to a friend." (Shweta Kukreja, verified LinkedIn creator, June 2026)
- **Buffer 52M+ post analysis (March 2026):** Carousels dominate LinkedIn engagement.

### Carousel Template (8–12 slides)

```
SLIDE 1 — THE HOOK
  One bold statement or question. "The [Number]-Part Framework I Use to [Achieve Specific Outcome]"
  → Creates "swipe to continue" curiosity gap. Never give away the answer on slide 1.

SLIDE 2 — THE CONTEXT
  "Why Most People Get [Topic] Wrong:" + 2–3 bullet points naming the common mistake
  → Validates the reader's frustration. They nod. They swipe.

SLIDES 3–10 — THE FRAMEWORK (one part per slide)
  • Bold headline: "Part [X]: [Name of Principle]"
  • 1–2 sentence explanation (conversational, not academic)
  • Visual element: diagram, icon, or contrast (before/after, wrong/right)
  • Every slide must end mid-thought so the viewer WANTS to swipe

SLIDE 11 — THE SUMMARY
  All framework parts on one savable slide
  → This is the slide users screenshot and save

SLIDE 12 — THE REPLY BAIT CTA
  "Which of these [X] parts is your weakest right now? Drop it in the comments — I'll share a deeper breakdown."
  → Drives conversation signals the algorithm rewards
```

### Accompanying Text Post (for carousel)

```
[Line 1 — conversational hook]: "I used to think [X]. I was wrong."
[Lines 2–4 — context]: Short paragraphs, white space between each. Read like a DM.
[Lines 5–6 — introduce framework]: "So I built a [X]-step system. It's in the carousel above. Here's the one part most people skip:"
[Lines 7–8 — one insight expanded]: Brief elaboration on the most counterintuitive part.
[Line 9 — reply bait]: "What's a belief about [topic] you held too long before changing it?"
```

### LinkedIn Text Post Benchmarks

- **Optimal length:** 1,000–2,000 characters for conversational text posts
- **Hook types that perform (from top 10% analysis):** number-led (31%), bold claim (27%), contrarian (18%)
- **Format distribution of top performers:** carousel (33%), image (29%), text only (22%)
- **Top 10% average post length:** 180–250 words
- **CTA patterns:** 45% mention newsletter; 5% use comment gates
- **Comment-to-reaction ratio of top performers:** 44%

## Instagram — 7 Content Formats (Deep Research, June 2026)

> **Research scope:** 15 sources, 52M+ posts analyzed (Buffer), 24.3M posts (Metricool), platform-wide tracking (Social Insider, Dash Social, Rival IQ), plus CreatorFlow, UpGrow, Outfame, Crescitaly, Sked Social. All data dated 2025–2026.
> **Full research file:** `~/.hermes/projects/point-clear-advisory/content-research/raw-research-instagram-2026-06-24.md`

### Platform Context

- **2026 is the 4th consecutive year of declining Instagram ER** — down ~20% since 2022 (UpGrow, Jun 2026)
- **Platform average ER:** 0.45–0.50% reach-based (UpGrow, Social Insider, Q1 2026)
- **Algorithm hierarchy (cross-sourced):** Saves > Shares > Comments > Likes (Buffer, Metricool, UpGrow)
- **Key 2026 changes:** Original content prioritized; hashtags less effective (-31.7% views when used); natural-language captions and SEO keywords now more important; early engagement (first 30 min) critical
- **Most views happen in first 3 days** after posting — timing and early engagement determine distribution
- **Follower tier variance:** Nano (1K-10K) = 2.19–6.23% ER; Mega (1M+) = 0.50–0.94% ER (Impulze.ai, Stack Influence via UpGrow)

### #1 Format: Educational / Value Carousel (5–10 slides)

- **Engagement:** 0.52–0.55% ER (reach-based) — #1 feed format on Instagram (Social Insider Q1 2026, CreatorFlow Jun 2026)
- **Saves:** 9x more than single-image posts (Metricool Jun 2026)
- **Engagement vs Reels:** 109% more engagement per impression (Buffer Mar 2026)
- **Reach rate:** 14.45% (Social Insider 2026)
- **YoY trend:** Most resilient format; engagement steady while other formats decline
- **Why it works:** Saves are the #1 algorithm signal in 2026. Carousels trigger saves because users bookmark them as reference content. Each swipe signals dwell time. Progressive disclosure creates "curiosity gap" that drives completion.

#### Template (7 slides optimal)

```
SLIDE 1 — THE HOOK
  "[Number] [things] I learned from [experience]"
  or "The [unexpected] truth about [topic]"
  → Bold claim or contrarian opening. Must stop the scroll.

SLIDES 2–6 — ONE IDEA PER SLIDE
  • Bold headline (max 10 words)
  • 1–2 sentence explanation
  • Visual: photo, chart, or screenshot
  • Each slide ends mid-thought → viewer wants to swipe

SLIDE 7 — THE CTA
  "Save this for when you need it."
  or "Share this with someone who needs to hear it."
  or "Comment [word] and I'll send you the full breakdown"

CAPTION:
  3–5 sentences expanding on the carousel topic.
  Include 1 question to drive comments.
  3–5 niche hashtags (declining effectiveness but still used).
  Character count: 1,200–1,500 (max 2,200).
```

#### What to Avoid
- Slide 1 is boring — if the hook doesn't stop the scroll, nobody swipes
- Too many slides (10+ sees drop-off after slide 7)
- Wall of text on slides — put depth in the caption
- No CTA on final slide — you earned the save, ask for it

### #2 Format: Short-Form Video Reel (15–90 seconds)

- **Engagement:** 0.50–0.52% ER (reach-based) (Social Insider Q1 2026)
- **Reach:** 30.81% reach rate — 1.36x more widely distributed than carousels (Buffer Mar 2026)
- **Interactions:** 4.3x more than single-image posts (Metricool Jun 2026)
- **Watch time:** 8.5 seconds average, doubled YoY (Metricool Jun 2026)
- **YoY growth:** +15% — only growing format on Instagram (Metricool Jun 2026)
- **Why it works:** Watch time + completion rate are the dominant ranking signals for Reels. Variable reward (short-form video) keeps the brain engaged. Shares via DM (up 12% YoY) signal high value. Algorithm distributes Reels to non-followers via Explore tab.

#### Template (15–30 seconds optimal)

```
SECOND 0–1: HOOK (visual + text overlay)
  "[Shocking statement] 🤯" or "Stop doing [common mistake]"
  Text overlay: Bold, 3–5 words, high contrast

SECOND 1–3: CONTEXT
  "Here's why this matters..."

SECOND 3–25: VALUE DELIVERY
  One point per 3–5 seconds. Jump cuts, no dead air.
  Text overlays for key points.
  Pattern: Problem → Agitation → Solution

SECOND 25–30: CTA
  "Follow for more [topic]"
  or "Save this for when you need it"

CAPTION:
  1–2 sentences + question to drive comments.
  Example: "Which of these surprised you most? 👇"
```

#### Technical Specs
- Resolution: 1080x1920 (9:16 vertical)
- Text safe zone: center 1080x1420 (avoid top/bottom UI)
- Audio: trending sounds get +15–20% distribution boost
- Subtitles: mandatory (80% watched without sound)

#### What to Avoid
- Long intro — if you don't hook in 0.5s, they scroll past
- Bad audio — audio is 50% of the Reel experience
- Reposting TikToks with watermark — Instagram deprioritizes watermarked content
- Overproducing — raw, authentic Reels outperform polished ones in 2026
- Ignoring watch time — if average watch time is under 3s, algorithm won't distribute

### #3 Format: Collaborative Post (Co-Authored via Collab Feature)

- **Reach:** 2.5x more than regular posts (Sked Social Apr 2026)
- **Follower growth:** 15–25% increase during collab campaigns (Outfame May 2026)
- **Engagement:** Higher than solo posts (audience overlap varies) (Sked Social 2026)
- **Available for:** Feed posts, carousels, and Reels
- **Why it works:** Collabs merge two audiences, creating higher initial engagement velocity. Instagram rewards cross-account interaction. Authority transfer — when a trusted creator co-authors with you, their trust transfers to you (halo effect). The algorithm interprets dual-audience engagement as a "valuable content" signal.

#### How to Execute

```
PRE-PRODUCTION:
  1. Identify partner with overlapping but non-identical audience (0.5x–3x your size)
  2. Agree on topic serving both audiences
  3. Decide format (carousel for education, Reel for reach)
  4. Create content together

POST STRUCTURE:
  - Same as standard carousel or Reel
  - Both accounts must approve the collab invite
  - Post appears on both profiles simultaneously

CAPTION TEMPLATE:
  "@partner and I teamed up because [reason].
  [2–3 sentences of value].
  Which perspective do you agree with — @partner's or mine? 👇"

TIMING:
  - Both accounts post simultaneously (collab feature handles this)
  - Both accounts engage in comments within first 30 minutes
```

#### What to Avoid
- Collabbing with accounts 10x your size — you'll be drowned out
- Not coordinating the CTA — both accounts should direct to the same action
- Ignoring the first hour — collabs need rapid initial engagement
- Collabbing too frequently — audience fatigue if every post is a collab

### #4 Format: Instagram Live (Solo & Collab Live Rooms)

- **Collab Live ER:** 8–12% (follower-based) vs 3–5% for solo streams (Outfame May 2026)
- **Follower growth:** 15–25% increase from collab lives (Outfame May 2026)
- **Comments during live:** 3–5x more than static posts (Outfame 2026)
- **Live Rooms:** Up to 4 participants, merging audiences and comments
- **Why it works:** Push notifications to all followers when you go live. Real-time presence creates "you had to be there" urgency. Parasocial bonding builds personal connection. Time spent watching live is the highest-quality engagement signal. Saved Lives get Replay distribution.

#### Template (30–60 minutes)

```
PRE-LIVE (30 min before):
  Story: "Going live with @partner at [time] to talk about [topic]. Set a reminder! 🔔"

MINUTE 0–3: INTRO
  "Hey everyone! I'm here with @partner. Today we're talking about [topic]. Drop a 🔥 if you're watching!"

MINUTE 3–20: MAIN CONTENT
  Co-hosted discussion, interview, or Q&A.
  Each host shares 2–3 key points.
  Ask audience: "What's your biggest challenge with [topic]?"

MINUTE 20–35: AUDIENCE Q&A
  Read comments aloud. Answer top questions.

MINUTE 35–40: CTA + WRAP
  "Follow @partner for [expertise]. Follow me for [expertise]."
  "Full recap drops as a Reel — stay tuned."
  Always save and repost as a Reel.
```

#### What to Avoid
- Going live without a plan — rambling kills viewership within 5 minutes
- No co-host or structure — solo lives are harder to maintain energy
- Ignoring comments — the whole point is real-time interaction
- Over 60 minutes without breaks — 30–45 min is optimal
- Not saving/reposting — always save the Live and post as a Reel

### #5 Format: Instagram Stories (Ephemeral 24h Content)

- **ER (sticker-based):** 5.4% (Social Insider 2026)
- **Reach:** 2–9% of followers (Social Insider / Storrito 2026)
- **Daily viewers:** 70% of users watch Stories daily (CreatorFlow Jun 2026)
- **Replies YoY:** +88% (Metricool Jun 2026)
- **Why it works:** Interactive sticker taps (polls, quizzes, questions) signal engagement. Reply rate is a strong DM signal that boosts overall profile ranking. Consistent Stories posting keeps your profile at the top of the Stories tray. Scarcity/urgency (24h lifespan) creates FOMO. Intimacy builds personal connection.

#### Template (3–7 stories per day)

```
STORY 1 — HOOK:
  "[Emoji] [Short statement]"
  or Behind-the-scenes photo/video
  Text: "Today I'm sharing..."

STORY 2–3 — VALUE:
  Quick tip, process clip, or insight
  Video: 15–30 seconds
  Add text overlay for sound-off viewers

STORY 4 — INTERACTIVE:
  Poll: "Do you agree? Yes / No"
  or Question sticker: "What do you want to know?"
  or Quiz: "True or false: [statement]"

STORY 5 — CTA:
  "Link in bio for [resource]"
  or "DM me [word] and I'll send you [thing]"
  or "New post is live — check it out"
```

#### What to Avoid
- Overproducing — Stories should feel raw and authentic
- No interactive elements — passive Stories don't signal engagement
- Posting 10+ stories in a row — users tap out after 5–7
- Ignoring replies — when someone replies, respond back

### #6 Format: Instagram Notes (Lightweight Text Updates)

- **Reach:** Followers only (no discovery) — appears at top of DM inbox
- **Max length:** 60 characters
- **Engagement type:** DM replies (no public ER metric)
- **YoY trend:** Growing as a "community" tool (Metricool Jun 2026)
- **Why it works:** Notes appear at the top of DMs, increasing profile visibility. Replies to Notes signal DM engagement (strong algorithm signal). Frequent, low-effort touchpoints build familiarity (mere exposure effect). Short text teasers create curiosity gap.

#### Template (2–5 per week)

```
MONDAY — VALUE TIP:
  "3 words that will save you $1,000"

WEDNESDAY — BEHIND-THE-SCENES:
  "Just finished [project]. Reel coming Friday."

FRIDAY — CTA:
  "New post is live. Link in bio 👆"

AS NEEDED — POLL/QUESTION:
  "What do you want me to cover next?"
```

#### What to Avoid
- Overusing — more than 1 per day feels spammy
- Long text — 60 character limit; respect it
- No follow-up — if you tease a post, deliver it
- Expecting reach — Notes are not a discovery tool

### #7 Format: Single-Image Post (Legacy — In Structural Decline)

- **ER:** 0.35% (reach-based), flat in Q1 2026 (Social Insider 2026)
- **YoY engagement change:** -45.98% (Metricool Jun 2026)
- **YoY reach change:** -21.96% (Metricool Jun 2026)
- **Reach rate:** 13.14% (UpGrow 2026)
- **Why it's declining:** Algorithm actively favors carousels and Reels. Single images offer fewer engagement triggers (no swipe, no watch time). Instagram's 2026 algorithm explicitly weights "time spent" — single images have near-zero dwell time.

#### When Single Images Still Work
- Photography accounts (the image IS the content)
- Announcements (product launches, events)
- Quote posts (text-on-image for viral quotes)
- Brand aesthetic (grid identity)

#### What to Avoid
- Using single images as your primary format — they're in structural decline
- Low-effort images — if you're going to use one, it must be exceptional
- Long captions as a crutch — the image must stand on its own

### #1 Format: Status Posts (Text Updates)

- **Engagement:** 0.20% avg ER (2025) — #1 format by engagement rate (Socialinsider, Mar 2026)
- **Q1 2026:** 0.13% — declining but still highest-rate format
- **Comments:** Generate the most comments per post across all page sizes (17 avg for 100K–1M page)
- **Likes:** For pages over 10K followers, status posts generate more likes than any other format
- **Shares:** Lower than albums but higher than images and Reels
- **Why it works:** Comments are the highest-weighted algorithm signal. Status posts naturally invite conversation — opinions, questions, reflections. Low-friction engagement (read → react → type). Feels human, not branded. Algorithm rewards content that sparks discussion between users.
- **Psychology:** Conversational norm + low-friction engagement. Users engage with status posts like they'd talk to a friend.

#### Template (80–200 characters optimal)
```
[Hook — opinion, contrarian take, or question]
[1–3 sentences of context or personal experience]
[Call to conversation — specific question or prompt]
```

**Template A — The Opinion Post:**
```
[Contrarian opinion about your industry]
I used to think [old belief] until [experience that changed my mind].
Now I believe [new belief] because [1–2 reasons].
Do you agree or am I off base? 👇
```

**Template B — The Question Post:**
```
What's one thing you stopped doing in your [work/business] that actually improved results?
I'll go first: [your answer]
Drop yours below 👇
```

#### What to Avoid
- Generic questions ("How's everyone doing?") — algorithm deprioritizes
- Engagement bait ("Like if you agree!") — explicitly penalized since 2018
- Over-promotion — status posts framed as sales messages kill the conversational norm
- Wall-of-text — over 500 characters without line breaks gets truncated
- No hook — starting with "So..." or "Just thinking..." wastes the scroll stop

### #2 Format: Image Posts (Single Photo)

- **Engagement:** 0.15% avg ER (stable 2024–2025) (Socialinsider, Mar 2026)
- **Median ER:** 5.20% (Buffer, 2025) — leads all formats by median metric
- **Organic reach:** 20–30% (FB Group Bulk Poster, Feb 2026)
- **Share performance:** High (albums lead, but single images also strong)
- **% of high-performing content:** 44.8% of all top posts across Facebook (SocialBee, 2026)
- **Why it works:** Picture superiority effect — humans engage with visuals 6× more than text. High dwell time (users pause to look). High save rate (reference material). Strong early engagement (stops the scroll faster than text). Images + carousels combined = nearly 60% of top posts.
- **Psychology:** Curiosity gap — compelling image makes users stop, then read caption for context.

#### Template
```
[Image: 1080×1080px (square) or 1080×1350px (4:5 portrait)]

Caption:
[1–3 sentence hook adding context to the image]
[Optional: data point, story, or insight]
[Question or conversation prompt]
```

**Template A — Product Showcase:**
```
[Image: Product in use or lifestyle context]
Caption: [What this is + who it's for] [Key benefit]. "Which color would you pick? 👇"
```

**Template B — Quote Card:**
```
[Image: Branded background + 1-sentence insight in clean typography]
Caption: "[Quote]" [1–2 sentences context] "What's a rule you live by? 👇"
```

**Template C — Behind-the-Scenes:**
```
[Image: Candid team photo, workspace, or process shot]
Caption: [What's happening] [Why it matters] "Ever had a day like this? 👇"
```

#### What to Avoid
- Low-resolution images — double penalty (algorithm + user ignore)
- Generic stock photos — authenticity wins in 2026
- Text-heavy images — Facebook may reduce reach
- No caption — misses conversation-driving opportunity
- Over-produced perfection — lo-fi imagery outperforming polished brand visuals

### #3 Format: Albums / Carousels (Multi-Image Posts)

- **Engagement:** 0.18% avg ER (stable 2024–2025) (Socialinsider, Mar 2026)
- **Shares:** Highest across all page sizes (Socialinsider, Mar 2026)
- **Views:** Highest across all page sizes (Socialinsider, Mar 2026)
- **Likes:** Highest for pages under 10K followers (8 avg for 1–5K page)
- **Organic reach:** 20–30% (FB Group Bulk Poster, Feb 2026)
- **Education vertical:** 5.2% engagement on albums (IQFluence, May 2026) — highest in dataset
- **Why it works:** Each swipe counts as an engagement signal (5 swipes = 5 signals). High dwell time (30–90 seconds). High save rate for educational/reference content. Completion bias — users who start swiping tend to finish. "Stoppable moment" in a fast-scrolling feed.
- **Psychology:** Completion bias + incremental revelation. Users feel rewarded for swiping through.

#### Template (3–7 images, 150–350 character caption)
```
[Image 1: Cover/hero — the most compelling visual]
[Image 2: Supporting detail or step 1]
[Image 3: Supporting detail or step 2]
[Image 4: Supporting detail or step 3]
[Image 5: CTA or summary]

Caption:
[Hook: "Here's what I learned from [experience]:" or "The complete guide to [topic]"]
[Brief context: 1–2 sentences]
[CTA: "Save this for later" or "Which step surprised you most? 👇"]
```

**Template A — Step-by-Step:**
```
[Image 1: "How to [achieve result] in [X] steps"]
[Image 2–4: One step per image]
[Image 5: Result/outcome photo]
Caption: "Most people try to [common mistake]. Here's the real path: [walk through]. Save this 🔖"
```

**Template B — Before & After:**
```
[Image 1: Before state]
[Image 2–3: Process details]
[Image 4: After state]
[Image 5: Close-up of result]
Caption: "[Time] ago: [before]. Now: [after]. Here's what changed: [3 bullets]"
```

**Template C — Photo Dump:**
```
[Image 1: Best shot — the cover]
[Image 2–6: Candid moments, details, BTS]
Caption: [Context for collection] [What makes it special] "Which photo is your favorite? 👇"
```

#### What to Avoid
- Weak cover image — if image 1 doesn't stop the scroll, nobody swipes
- Too many images — over 7 sees drop-off
- No narrative — random images without a story feel like spam

### #1 Format: Question Posts

- **Engagement:** 6.14% median ER — #1 format on Threads (SociaVault 2026)
- **vs platform average:** +62%
- **Reply rate:** Highest of all content types
- **Best niches:** Tech/AI (5.24% overall), Media (4.97%), Politics (4.48%)
- **Why it works:** Replies are the #1 algorithm signal. Questions that invite personal experience or position-taking generate the most replies. Information gap + self-relevance bias.
- **Anti-pattern:** NOT for B2B/SaaS audiences (2.31% overall ER) — questions don't overcome audience mismatch.

#### Template

```
[One line of context or stakes that makes the question matter]

[Specific question that invites personal experience or a position — NOT yes/no]

[Optional: "Here's what I've seen: [your short take]" — post as reply to yourself]
```

**Character count:** 50–200 characters main post. Reply-to-self: under 150 characters.

#### High-performing hook patterns
- "I've been doing X for Y years. The thing nobody tells you is [blank]. What's been your experience?"
- "90% of [audience] believe [common belief]. But what if [contrarian premise]? Am I wrong?"
- "Just saw [observation]. Genuinely curious — does anyone else see this?"

#### What to Avoid
- Yes/no questions — too easy to dismiss
- Overly broad questions ("What do you think about AI?") — no hook, no urgency
- Rhetorical questions where you've already answered them
- Questions that feel like market research
- Neglecting to reply to responses — creator replies +42%
- Engagement bait phrasing ("Double tap if you agree!") — algorithm penalizes

### #2 Format: Hot Takes / Constructive Contrarian

- **Engagement:** 5.87% median ER — #2 format (SociaVault 2026)
- **vs platform average:** +55%
- **Reply-to-like ratio:** 1:3.5 for top performers
- **Best niches:** Politics/Current Events (3.12% reply rate), Tech/AI
- **CRITICAL DISTINCTION:** "Hot take" ≠ "negativity." Threads algorithm penalizes combative/angry content (-58% reach per Teract). A successful hot take is: grounded in evidence, constructive (adds new information or angle), and framed as a position others can engage WITH, not attack.
- **Why it works:** Replies + quote-reposts. Confirmation bias + reactance + social identity. Threads weights reply DEPTH more than raw volume — a position generating 20 thoughtful replies outperforms one generating 200 angry retweets.

#### Template

```
Hot take: [your specific, defensible claim]

[One piece of evidence, data point, or experience that grounds it]

[Optional: "Change my mind" — only if you genuinely welcome informed pushback]
```

**Character count:** 100–300 characters. The take itself: under 100 characters.

#### High-performing variations
- "Unpopular opinion: [claim]. Here's why."
- "I used to believe [X]. Then [happened]. Now I think [Y]."
- "The [industry] consensus says [X]. I think that's wrong because [Y]."
- "[Thing] is overrated. [Alternative] is underrated. Here's the data:"

#### What to Avoid
- ❌ X-style dunking — "Imagine believing [stupid thing]" — algorithm penalizes
- ❌ Controversy without substance — Threads rewards grounded positions, not vibes
- ❌ Takes that are too obvious — "Hard work pays off" generates zero replies
- ❌ Not engaging with replies — the conversation IS the content
- ❌ Cross-posting from X — direct reposts underperform (SociaVault FAQ)
- ❌ Engagement bait wrapping — "If you agree, repost it" — flagged as manipulation
- ✅ **DO:** Be constructive, add evidence, invite genuine debate

#### What to Avoid

- Long intros — hook must land in 3 seconds

### #4 Format: Multi-Part Threads (Lists / Threaded Content)

- **Engagement:** 4.92% median ER (SociaVault 2026)
- **vs platform average:** +30%
- **Save rate:** Reference-level (users save for later)
- **Why it works:** Saves + profile views. Zeigarnik effect (incomplete tasks create tension). Commitment bias. Each part generates its own conversation signals.

#### Template

**Part 1:**
```
[Number] [things/learnings/steps] from [experience] 🧵

1/[Number]: [First insight — must be valuable standalone]
```

**Middle parts:**
```
[Number]/[Number]: [One specific insight]

[Brief example or data point]

[Line creating tension: "But here's where it gets interesting…"]
```

**Final part:**
```

[Specific question that could ONLY come from this thread]
```

**Per part:** 100–300 characters. Total thread: 500–2,000 characters. Optimal: 3–7 parts.

#### What to Avoid
- Part 1 hook that doesn't establish value — if it doesn't land, thread dies
- Parts too long — must be scannable in 10–15 seconds each
- Generic endings — "What do you think?" is a dead end
- No visual element — even one image boosts engagement significantly
- Posting all parts at once — space 30–60 seconds apart
- Writing 10+ part threads — completion drops sharply after 7

- **Engagement:** 4.71% median ER (SociaVault 2026)
- **vs platform average:** +24%
- **Creator reply lift:** +42% — HIGHEST of any platform (Buffer 2026)

#### Template

```
[Your unique take — NOT "Great post!" or "This!"]

[Data point, experience, or perspective that adds something new]

[Optional: question that continues the conversation]
```

**Character count:** 50–300 characters.

#### High-performing reply patterns

- "Most people will miss [nuance]. Here's why it matters:"
- "The [industry] angle on this is:"

#### What to Avoid
- "Great post!" / "This!" / "🔥" — algorithm penalizes as low-effort
- Derailing the conversation — must relate to original topic
- Being long-winded — if reply needs 500+ characters, write your own post
- Not replying to replies — conversation depth = distribution
