# The Adams Aside — Surreal Humour in Practical Writing

## What it is

A controlled technique for injecting Douglas Adams-style surreal, absurdist humour into otherwise serious practical writing (LinkedIn posts, newsletters, B2B content). Not "being funny" — letting the reader's brain do a small double-take at exactly the right moment.

## When to use it

- The user explicitly requests it (as a "smidgen," "tiniest bit," "not heavy-handed")
- The topic is practical/serious but has room for a moment of levity
- The stakes are low enough that humour doesn't undermine gravity

## When NOT to use it

- Serious topics: grief, failure, financial loss, health crises
- When the humour would arrive at the reader's expense
- When the surrounding tone is already light (don't stack)
- More than twice per post — quality over quantity

## The three moves

### 1. The aside that undercuts seriousness
Drop a perfectly-timed absurd observation into the middle of something that had been building momentum. Usually a single line in brackets or a short paragraph after a serious point.

**Example pattern:**
> "The algorithm now penalizes low-quality volume. (It also, apparently, has opinions about your font choices.)"

The absurd element arrives without setup. The reader wasn't expecting it. That's what makes it work.

### 2. The deadpan escalation
Take a logical idea one step further than expected. Just far enough to be funny. Not so far it becomes nonsense.

**Example pattern:**
> "Dark social drives 84% of B2B content sharing. The other 16% happens in a Slack channel you're not in, discussing whether your content is any good. You'll never see those messages. Neither will your analytics tool."

### 3. The reframing
Present a familiar concept through an unexpected lens — usually by taking a metaphor literally or treating the abstract as concrete.

**Example pattern:**
> "Your LinkedIn analytics tell you 500 people saw your post. What they don't tell you is that 499 of them scrolled past in the time it takes to sneeze. One of them stopped. That's the one who matters."

## Rules

1. **Once per post maximum.** The reader should be surprised, not expecting it.
2. **No setup.** Don't warn the reader something funny is coming. Just drop it.
3. **Earned by context.** The aside must emerge naturally from the point being made, not a non-sequitur.
4. **British sensibility.** Adams' humour is dry, not slapstick. Keep it dry.
5. **If in doubt, leave it out.** A post without an Adams aside is fine. A post with a forced one isn't.
6. **The user is the judge.** If they say a line doesn't work, remove it without argument. Humour is subjective.

## How to implement in voice.md

When building voice.md for a user who wants this technique, add a line under **Tone**:

> The Adams aside: a single-line surreal observation that undercuts seriousness at exactly the right moment. Used once per post maximum. Never forced. If the topic doesn't support it, it stays out.

And under **What this voice never does**:

> Never attempts Adams-style humour on serious topics (grief, failure, financial loss). The absurdity only works when the stakes are low enough to undercut.
